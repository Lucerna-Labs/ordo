# Marketing Foundations

## Message structure

- Lead with the problem or desired outcome, not internal features.
- Show why this matters now.
- Make the value specific enough that the right audience feels seen.
- Support claims with proof, examples, or a concrete mechanism.

## Positioning basics

- Audience first: who is this for?
- Pain first: what friction or desire is active?
- Differentiation next: why this option over the default alternative?
- Action last: what should happen after the message lands?

## Channel adjustment

- Ads need fast clarity and a sharp hook.
- Landing pages need promise, proof, and friction reduction.
- Email needs relevance, timing, and a single clear next step.
- Social copy needs compression, tone, and a reason to stop scrolling.
